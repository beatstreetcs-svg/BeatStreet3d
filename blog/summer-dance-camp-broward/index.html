Summer camp

What to look for in a Broward summer dance camp

A good summer dance camp gives kids structure, movement, creativity, and social connection. The right fit should also make scheduling and pricing clear for parents.

Check the camp day structure

Ask how the day balances dance lessons, movement activities, creative projects, breaks, and pickup routines. A full-day camp should have enough variety to keep kids engaged.

Understand pricing and deposits

Parents should know weekly rates, daily options, paid-in-full discounts, deposit rules, and what is included. BeatStreet 3D lists weekly, daily, and multi-week camp options so families can plan ahead.

Look for a studio environment

Dance camp is especially valuable when kids are surrounded by instructors who understand movement, performance, and confidence-building. A studio setting can help campers explore dance even if they are new to classes.
